Data Overview

The data we are exploring here is World Exports Data, which includes various indicators related to international trade performance. These indicators cover the following:

  • Exports of goods and services (% of GDP): Represents the value of all goods and market services provided to the rest of the world, including merchandise, transport, financial, and other services.
  • Fuel exports (% of merchandise exports): Includes commodities related to mineral fuels, lubricants, and related materials.
  • Energy imports, net (% of energy use): Net energy imports calculated as the difference between energy use and domestic production.
  • High-technology exports (% of manufactured exports): Includes products with high R&D intensity, such as in aerospace, computers, pharmaceuticals, scientific instruments, and electrical machinery.

DATA SOURCE:

  • Exports of goods and services - World Bank National Accounts data and OECD National Accounts data files.
  • Fuel exports - WITS Platform: World Bank staff estimates based on Comtrade data maintained by the United Nations Statistics Division.
  • Energy imports - IEA Statistics: Â© OECD/IEA 2014, subject to IEA terms.
  • High-technology exports - United Nations Comtrade Database through the WITS platform.

This dataset includes information for 265 countries, representing various regions of the world. Each country has export values recorded from the year 1960 to 2022, allowing for a comprehensive analysis of global export trends and performance across different time periods.
