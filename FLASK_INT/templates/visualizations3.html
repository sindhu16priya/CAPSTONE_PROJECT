Analysis

The above pie charts depict the average export data for each of the export categories across different income groups. Below are the key observations:

1. Fuel Exports: The highest contribution comes from the lower-middle-income group (31.2%). There is not much difference between the high-income group (26.1%) and the upper-middle-income group (27.3%), both of which also dominate fuel exports.

2. Exports of Goods and Services: The high-income group leads with 39.6%. There is a slight difference between the lower-middle-income group (21.8%) and the upper-middle-income group (24.9%).

3. Energy Imports: Significant differences are observed among the four income groups. The high-income group leads with 39%, followed by the upper-middle-income group (29.8%) and the lower-middle-income group (29.3%). The low-income group contributes the least, with only 10.9%.

4. High-tech Exports: This category is predominantly dominated by the high-income group (53.4%), followed by the upper-middle-income group (20.4%). The lower-middle-income and low-income groups have similar contributions, at 13.1% each.
